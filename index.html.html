Pillar 01 · Membership

Membership Growth — New Tactics Only

The website, free trials, and Instagram posts are already running. These are the approaches NLCC isn't yet using.

👩‍🏏
Women's Cricket — Active Outreach Campaign
The page exists and @nlcc_women is live — but there's no active recruitment targeting women who aren't already looking
⭐ Priority
📌 The Gap
NLCC's women's content is strong but passive — it waits for people to find it. The women who would love NLCC don't know it exists. They need to be found where they already are, not just presented with a landing page.
Find New Audiences
  • Partner with 3–5 local women's fitness groups, running clubs, or netball teams in N4/N8/N10 for Instagram story swaps. Zero cost, precisely the right demographic.
  • Post in Nextdoor and North London community Facebook groups (Crouch End Community, Hornsey & Crouch End groups — tens of thousands of local members). NLCC has no presence here currently.
  • Get listed on Mumsnet Local North London — mothers who play or want their daughters to play. A free listing that stays live permanently.
Convert Interest to Members
  • Launch a Women's Ambassador Programme: ask each existing women's member to personally invite one friend this season using a shareable WhatsApp template NLCC provides. Peer referral consistently outperforms any paid campaign.
  • Create a women's WhatsApp social group (separate from squad admin). Strong off-pitch community is the #1 retention driver in recreational sport.
  • End-of-season recognition: Best Newcomer and Most Improved awards. Minimal cost, strong loyalty signal.
🧒
Junior — School Outreach (Not Currently Done)
Proactive pipeline beyond the website
New
  • Contact 8–10 local primary schools Jan–Feb with a single well-crafted paragraph about Allstars/Dynamos. Schools actively seek quality activities to recommend. One email per school, no follow-up needed.
  • Ask to be included in school newsletters — most have a community section. One placement reaches hundreds of families at no cost.
  • Provide existing junior families a copy-paste WhatsApp message to share in their class parent groups each new term. Removes all friction from word-of-mouth referral.
🏏
Men's — Formalise the Referral System
Word-of-mouth made structured and incentivised
New
  • Launch a Member Referral Programme: any existing member who recruits a new full-fee member gets their next match fee waived. Simple, low cost, proven model across amateur clubs.
  • Post "nets open to newcomers" in local Facebook groups and Nextdoor 2–3 times before the season. The website says this — take it to where people are actually browsing.
  • Target local workplaces and offices in N4/N8 with a short message about social/corporate cricket. A single WhatsApp to the right person can unlock multiple memberships.
Pillar 02 · Revenue

Revenue — Untapped Income Streams

The summer camp is strong. These are revenue channels NLCC is not currently running.

📧
Camp Email Marketing — Capture Demand Early
Booking forms exist but there's no proactive email outreach to member families
Quick Win
  • Add a Mailchimp "register interest" sign-up for families not yet ready to commit: "Be first to know about Summer 2026 Camp dates." Converts passive interest into warm leads before bookings open.
  • Send a dedicated camp announcement email to all Pitchero member families 8–10 weeks before summer. NLCC doesn't currently do this. Parents book early when prompted — they forget without a nudge.
  • Add an early-bird incentive (10% off full-week bookings made before 1 June) to reward advance commitment and improve capacity planning.
🎉
Fundraising — Currently Zero
No events, no JustGiving, no structured fundraising exists
New Revenue
  • Annual Club Quiz Night (Jan/Feb off-season) — fully delegated to one volunteer. £10/head + raffle. Target £600–£1,000 net. Low overhead, high community value.
  • End-of-season Awards Dinner — ticket sales, raffle, auction. One volunteer owns it. Builds community and generates income at the same time.
  • JustGiving page with a specific goal (e.g. "Junior Nets Upgrade" or "Women's Kit Fund"). Specific goals convert far better than general asks. Promote via email and Instagram once.
💼
New Sponsorship Tiers — No Documented Offer Currently Exists
Sponsors are on the homepage but there's no package to actively sell to new sponsors
£500–£1,500/yr potential
Tiered Sponsorship Menu
  • Kit Logo Sponsor — logo on playing shirts for one section, £500/year.
  • Digital Sponsor — logo in every Instagram post footer and email footer, £750/year.
  • Headline Sponsor — kit + digital + dedicated spotlight post + mention at all events, £1,500/year.
  • A one-page PDF "Sponsorship Pack" makes the ask easy for any committee member. Sponsors can't buy something they can't see clearly.
Finding New Sponsors
  • Target businesses that parents of junior members own or manage — they already have an emotional connection to the club. Ask coaches and section leads to name 3–5 each.
  • Approach Crouch End high street independents: cafés, estate agents, gyms, dentists. Frame it as a local marketing investment reaching 1,173 Instagram followers and a member email list, not a charity ask.
  • Upsell existing sponsors to the new digital tier at renewal — position it as expanded visibility at a defined price.
🏫
Easter Camp & After-School Programme
Extending the successful camp model — infrastructure already exists
Untapped
  • NLCC runs a strong 7-week summer camp. There is currently no Easter camp or after-school programme. Both represent additional revenue using existing infrastructure (coaches, venue, booking system) with minimal extra setup cost.
  • A 3–4 day Easter camp at current pricing (£45/day, £160 for the week) for the same families is a natural, low-risk extension of what already works.
  • An after-school session (e.g. Tuesday/Thursday 4–6pm, term-time) at £8–£10/session for ages 7–13 creates weekly recurring revenue and deepens junior member retention throughout the year.
Pillar 03 · Digital & SEO

Digital — Specific Gaps in an Otherwise Good Foundation

The website structure is solid. These are targeted improvements that will drive meaningful organic growth.

🔍
SEO — Missing Content Signals
Good structure but no content velocity — the key long-term ranking driver
High Impact
Content Gaps
  • There is no blog or news section on the website. Publishing 1–2 posts per month (match reports, camp previews, member stories) builds domain authority — the most important long-term SEO lever for a local club. Competitors who blog will outrank NLCC over time regardless of page structure.
  • The camps page is titled "Summer Cricket Camp 2025" — update to 2026 and sharpen the keyword: "Summer Cricket Camp North London 2026, Ages 5–13." Minor change, immediate SEO improvement.
  • No "About Us / Our Story" page exists. A page covering the club's history (founded in Crouch End), values, and community role adds credibility and internal link structure that search engines reward.
Technical Improvements
  • Install Google Analytics 4 (free) if not active. Without it NLCC cannot know which pages convert visitors to enquiries — arguably the most useful data the club doesn't have.
  • Submit to free local directories: Haringey and Islington council leisure directories, Mumsnet Local, Time Out London sport. Each is a free backlink that improves Google ranking for local searches.
  • Add FAQ schema markup to the existing FAQ section — a small technical addition (one plugin or 10 lines of code) that can earn a featured snippet position in Google, appearing above all paid ads for "cricket club north london" queries.
📧
Email Marketing — Entirely Absent
No newsletter, no campaigns, no automated sequences beyond Pitchero operational emails
High ROI Gap
💡 Recommendation: Mailchimp Free Tier
Up to 500 contacts, 1,000 sends/month — completely free. Use for campaigns (camp promos, fundraising, welcome sequences). Keep Pitchero for squad/operational messages. NLCC owns a direct communication channel that no algorithm can suppress.
  • 3 automated sequences to build once: (1) New Member Welcome — sent automatically on registration, introduces the club and key contacts. (2) Camp Booking Confirmation + future dates prompt. (3) End-of-Season Survey + Renewal Prompt.
  • Monthly member newsletter during the season: fixture highlights, upcoming camps, one sponsor mention. Keeps NLCC visible during the off-season when members drift.
  • Capture all Google Form camp bookings into an email list. Currently these contacts are collected but never re-marketed to. Every family who books camp should automatically receive next summer's camp announcement.
📱
Facebook — Completely Untapped
NLCC has no Facebook presence despite it being the primary platform for parents and local community decision-making
Untapped
  • NLCC's Instagram is strong but skews young. Parents making decisions about junior cricket, and adults joining recreational cricket, are primarily active on Facebook — not Instagram. This is a different audience going unreached.
  • Create an NLCC Facebook page. Repurpose the same Instagram content — same photos, same announcements. Minimal extra effort, entirely new audience.
  • Join and post in local community groups: "Crouch End Community", "Crouch End Parents", "Things to Do in Crouch End", "Haringey Families." These groups have 10,000–50,000 members and camp/activity posts frequently generate dozens of organic referrals.

Instagram Reels — The Biggest Untapped Growth Tool
NLCC's feed is almost entirely static images — Reels get 3–5x the organic reach
Highest Impact
  • Instagram's algorithm heavily prioritises Reels in discovery (Explore page). Static posts reach primarily existing followers. Reels reach new audiences who don't yet follow NLCC — the people the club needs to recruit. This single format change is the most impactful social media shift available.
  • Create a "Content WhatsApp" group with 6–8 players across all sections. Anyone submits raw clips from match days or training. The marketing lead (or Rahul Gill) reviews and edits. Distributes the creation burden; nobody feels obligated to post constantly.
  • Reel formats that work for cricket clubs: batting/bowling technique clips, a 30-second "Meet the Player" format, match-day highlights, junior kids celebrating wickets, behind-the-scenes training. None require expensive production — a phone shot well is enough.
🔗
Fix the Two-Account Split
@northlondoncricket and @nlcc_women are not connected — the audience is fragmented
Structural Fix
  • Women's content on @nlcc_women (616 followers) is invisible to the main account's 1,173 followers. The two audiences never meet. Every women's post should tag @northlondoncricket and vice versa — cross-pollinating both audiences at zero cost.
  • Add the @nlcc_women handle to the main account bio. Currently there is no link between them in the bio.
  • Consider consolidating to one account with clear section labelling (e.g. post captions starting with "👩 Women's:" or "🧒 Juniors:") — a single stronger account grows faster and is easier to manage than two separate ones.
🤝
Sponsor Content Activation
Sponsors are on the website but not activated through social content
Protects Renewals
  • One dedicated sponsor spotlight post per sponsor per year. Send for approval first — sponsors who are featured share it to their own audience, extending NLCC's reach for free.
  • Build a sponsor mention rotation into the content calendar: every 4th–5th post references a sponsor. Document it so it becomes automatic, not an afterthought.
  • Produce a one-page annual sponsor report showing follower growth, post reach data, and member numbers. Makes renewal conversations easy and sponsor retention far more likely.
